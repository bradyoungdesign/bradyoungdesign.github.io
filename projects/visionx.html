VisionX

Date

2024

Platform

Responsive Website

Services

UI/UX Designer

Seeing into the future

VisionX is an optometry specialist in Parnell who recently went through a new store renovation and was looking for an updated website to reflect the updated style and the recent branding changes.

The problem

VisionX's existing digital presence lacked visual alignment with their premium in-store experience and suffered from a non-intuitive user flow for new and returning customers.

To align the online presence of VisionX with the physical renovations of the main branch, they wanted to design and develop a new responsive website with a "modern and clean" aesthetic that provided an easy navigating experience.

In an interview with the owner Richard, he revealed some critical pain points that he had previously experienced with websites, including the outdated look and mobile experience.

Richard expressed that although the primary method of communication with his customers is through social media and in-person, he would like to be able to advertise new promotions on his website.

He also stated that the website had an existing web store that he would like integrated as an extension of this project at a future date.

Issues with the information hierarchy in the hero section and unnecessary initial flow through to the main content of the website were evident in click-throughs and confirmed through user testing.

Goals

After a preliminary interview with Richard, staff of VisionX and customers of the store, the scope of the project, the reasons for building the new website and how it was going to be built were defined.

The User Need (UX)

Effortless Navigation: Users need a frictionless, intuitive way to find essential store details and services without digging through menus.

Authentic Engagement: Customers want to understand VisionX's values, promotions, and unique selling points to validate their purchasing decisions.

The Business Value (ROI)

Increased Conversions: Reducing interaction cost and clicks-to-information directly lowers drop-off rates and drives foot traffic to the store.

Customer Acquisition: A modernised, unique design based on the physical store builds brand equity differentiates VisionX from competitors, and converts casual visitors into new customers.

Accessibility

(WCAG): For an optometry specialist website, web accessibility is crucial.The website should be designed for color contrast, readability, and font sizing to ensure low-vision users can easily navigate the site.
